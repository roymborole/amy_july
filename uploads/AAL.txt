The 1.7% increase in passenger revenue was driven by an 8.0% increase in capacity (ASMs), offset by a 6.3% decrease in passenger yield, indicating volume growth rather than price increases drove the topline.

o Strong revenue growth can indicate rising demand or successful market expansion. Slowing or declining revenue might suggest market saturation or economic downturns – does the above relate to the stock and if so how?
Management attributed the weak Q2 2024 revenue performance to "weak industry pricing conditions in light of significantly increased industry capacity, as well as softness in customer bookings due in part to certain commercial initiatives that we deployed, many of which were reversed late in the quarter." This suggests industry overcapacity and American's own strategic missteps temporarily weighed on revenue, rather than an economic downturn. The stock could be impacted if investors are concerned about American's ability to fill its increased capacity profitably.
