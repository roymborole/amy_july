Management Discussion and Analysis:

o How has the company's revenue changed compared to the previous quarter and the same quarter last year?

Net sales decreased by $256 million or 7% in Q3 FY2024 compared to Q3 FY2023. Excluding currency benefits and lower raw material pass-through costs, net sales decreased by around 6% driven by an unfavorable price/mix impact of 3% and 4% lower volumes.

Compared to the previous quarter (Q2 FY2024), net sales increased sequentially, reflecting improved volumes due to the abatement of customer destocking across many end markets.

o Is the growth in sales driven by volume or price increases? 

The year-over-year decrease in sales for Q3 FY2024 was primarily driven by lower volumes of around 4%, with an unfavorable price/mix impact of around 3%. Sequentially from Q2 FY2024, sales growth was driven by higher volumes as destocking abated.

o Strong revenue growth can indicate rising demand or successful market expansion. Slowing or declining revenue might suggest market saturation or economic downturns – does the above relate to the stock and if so how?

The declining year-over-year revenue appears to be driven by softer consumer and customer demand amidst broader economic conditions, including higher inflation and interest rates impacting consumption. The sequential improvement from Q2 FY2024 suggests some stabilization in demand as destocking abated.

These revenue trends could impact investor sentiment on the stock. Declining revenues raise concerns about the company's ability to grow, while the sequential improvement provides some optimism that conditions may be stabilizing. However, the ongoing macroeconomic uncertainties create risks that could further pressure revenues and earnings.

o How have these margins trended over recent quarters?

Gross profit margin increased from 18.4% in Q3 FY2023 to 20.3% in Q3 FY2024, driven by cost savings initiatives.

Operating income margin increased from 7.7% in Q3 FY2023 to 9.0% in Q3 FY2024.

Net income margin increased from 4.8% in Q3 FY2023 to 5.5% in Q3 FY2024.

The year-over-year margin expansion appears to be driven by the company's cost management efforts offsetting the impacts of lower volumes and unfavorable pricing.

o Increasing profit margins suggest better cost management or pricing power, while declining margins may indicate rising costs or competitive pressures – does this apply to the company at all?

The increasing profit margins for Amcor in Q3 FY2024 suggest that the company's cost management initiatives, including its 2023 Restructuring Plan, are having a positive impact in offsetting the negative effects of lower volumes and unfavorable pricing. This indicates Amcor has been able to flex its cost base and enhance profitability despite the challenging market conditions.

However, the company continues to face inflationary pressures, particularly in areas like labor costs. Its ability to sustain or further expand margins will depend on the effectiveness of its pricing actions and continued cost discipline.
