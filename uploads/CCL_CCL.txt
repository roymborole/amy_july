Management Discussion and Analysis (MD&A):

o How has the company's revenue changed compared to the previous quarter and the same quarter last year?
Passenger ticket revenues increased by $613 million or 20% to $3,754 million in Q2 2024 compared to $3,141 million in Q2 2023. This was driven by continued strength in demand, 5.4% capacity increase, and 5.6 percentage point increase in occupancy.
Onboard and other revenues increased by $257 million or 15% to $2,027 million in Q2 2024 from $1,770 million in Q2 2023, driven by capacity increase, higher occupancy, and higher onboard spending.

o Is the growth in sales driven by volume or price increases? 
The growth in passenger ticket revenues was driven by both volume (capacity increase and higher occupancy) and price increases due to continued strong demand. The onboard revenue growth was also driven by volume factors as well as higher onboard spending by guests.

o Strong revenue growth can indicate rising demand or successful market expansion. Slowing or declining revenue might suggest market saturation or economic downturns – does the above relate to the stock and if so how?
The strong revenue growth indicates rising demand for cruises, successful return to operations post-pandemic, and effective yield management strategies. This positive momentum could support the company's stock performance if it continues, as it demonstrates the company's ability to capitalize on market opportunities and drive profitability.
