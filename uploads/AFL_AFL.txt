Q2 2024 Macroeconomic Analysis for Aflac Incorporated
Date: August 1, 2024

o How has the company's revenue changed compared to the previous quarter and the same quarter last year?
- Net earned premiums decreased 16.9% compared to Q2 2023, primarily due to the internal reinsurance transaction with Aflac Re and limited-pay products reaching premium paid-up status at Aflac Japan.
- Total revenues decreased 0.7% compared to Q2 2023.

o Is the growth in sales driven by volume or price increases? 
The growth in sales appears to be primarily driven by volume rather than price increases. Aflac Japan's new annualized premium sales increased 4.5% in yen terms in Q2 2024 compared to Q2 2023, driven by sales of its new life insurance product Tsumitasu. Aflac U.S. new annualized premium sales increased 2.0% in Q2 2024 compared to Q2 2023, largely driven by group life, absence management and disability products.

o Strong revenue growth can indicate rising demand or successful market expansion. Slowing or declining revenue might suggest market saturation or economic downturns – does the above relate to the stock and if so how?
Aflac's revenue growth has slowed, with total revenues decreasing 0.7% year-over-year in Q2 2024. This appears to be primarily driven by the internal reinsurance transaction and limited-pay products maturing at Aflac Japan, rather than market saturation or economic downturns. Aflac continues to see growth in new sales volumes in both Japan and the U.S. The slowing topline growth could raise some concerns for investors about Aflac's ability to drive future revenue growth, potentially impacting the stock price.

o What are the company's gross, operating, and net profit margins?
Aflac does not explicitly disclose gross profit margins. Key profit metrics disclosed include:
- Pretax adjusted profit margin (adjusted earnings / adjusted revenues) at Aflac Japan was 35.3% in Q2 2024, up from 30.4% in Q2 2023
- Pretax adjusted profit margin at Aflac U.S. was 22.7% in Q2 2024, up slightly from 22.2% in Q2 2023  

o How have these margins trended over recent quarters?
Aflac Japan's pretax adjusted profit margin increased in Q2 2024 compared to the prior year quarter, driven by lower expense and benefit ratios.
Aflac U.S.'s pretax adjusted profit margin increased slightly in Q2 2024 compared to Q2 2023, due to higher revenues and lower expenses offsetting higher benefits.

o Increasing profit margins suggest better cost management or pricing power, while declining margins may indicate rising costs or competitive pressures – does this apply to the company at all?
The increasing profit margins at both Aflac Japan and Aflac U.S. in Q2 2024 suggest improved cost management and expense efficiency efforts. There is no indication of significant pricing power driving the margin expansion. The margins do not appear to be impacted by rising costs or competitive pressures currently.
