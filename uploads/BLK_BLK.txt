Q2 2024 Macroeconomic Analysis for BlackRock, Inc.

Date: August 6, 2024

Revenue:
- How has the company's revenue changed compared to the previous quarter and the same quarter last year?
BlackRock's total revenue was $4.805 billion in Q2 2024, an increase of 8% from $4.463 billion in Q2 2023. Compared to Q1 2024 revenue of $4.728 billion, Q2 2024 revenue increased by 1.6%.

- Is the growth in sales driven by volume or price increases? 
The revenue growth was primarily driven by higher investment advisory and administration fees from organic base fee growth and the positive impact of higher markets on average AUM. Performance fees of $164 million increased 39% from $118 million in Q2 2023, reflecting higher revenue from liquid alternatives and long-only products. Technology services revenue grew 10% year-over-year to $395 million.

- Strong revenue growth can indicate rising demand or successful market expansion. Slowing or declining revenue might suggest market saturation or economic downturns – does the above relate to the stock and if so how?
BlackRock's strong 8% year-over-year revenue growth in Q2 2024 indicates continued rising demand for its products and services across its institutional and retail client base globally. The growth was broad-based across investment advisory fees, performance fees, and technology services. This robust top-line growth should be viewed positively by investors, as it demonstrates BlackRock's ability to capitalize on market opportunities despite potential economic headwinds.

Profitability:  
- What are the company's gross, operating, and net profit margins?
BlackRock does not report traditional gross profit margins. Its operating income was $1.800 billion in Q2 2024, representing an operating margin of 37.5%. Net income attributable to BlackRock was $1.495 billion.

- How have these margins trended over recent quarters?
BlackRock's Q2 2024 operating margin of 37.5% increased from 36.2% in Q2 2023 and 35.0% in Q1 2024. The year-over-year margin expansion was driven by the revenue growth outpacing expense growth.

- Increasing profit margins suggest better cost management or pricing power, while declining margins may indicate rising costs or competitive pressures – does this apply to the company at all?
BlackRock's increasing operating margins over recent quarters suggest it has been able to effectively manage costs and leverage operating scale as AUM and revenue have grown. The margin improvement indicates BlackRock has been able to maintain its pricing power despite competitive pressures in the asset management industry.

Earnings:
- Has the EPS increased or decreased compared to previous periods? 
Diluted EPS was $9.99 in Q2 2024, up 10% from $9.06 in Q2 2023 and up 4% from $9.62 in Q1 2024.

- How does the EPS compare to analyst expectations?
BlackRock's Q2 2024 diluted EPS of $9.99 exceeded the consensus analyst estimate of around $9.60.

Yes, BlackRock generated $2.015 billion of operating cash flow in the first half of 2024, excluding the impact of cash flows from consolidated investment products.

In the first half of 2024, BlackRock used its cash for $1.553 billion in dividend payments, $1.153 billion in share repurchases, $1.0 billion of debt repayment, and $74 million for an acquisition. It also raised $2.979 billion from new long-term debt issuances to fund a pending acquisition.

As of June 30, 2024, BlackRock had $9.857 billion in borrowings and $40.102 billion in stockholders' equity, representing a debt-to-equity ratio of approximately 0.25x.

BlackRock's debt level increased from $7.918 billion at December 31, 2023 to $9.857 billion at June 30, 2024, primarily due to the new $3 billion debt issuance in March 2024 to fund an acquisition, partially offset by repayment of $1 billion of maturing debt.

The capital expenditures appear to be primarily for maintenance and technology investments to support BlackRock's operations and services.

Competitive Position:
- How is the company performing relative to its peers and the overall industry?
BlackRock remains the largest asset manager globally. Its 8% year-over-year revenue growth in Q2 2024 outpaced the mid-single digit growth rates of many traditional asset manager peers. BlackRock continues to gain market share in areas like ETFs.

- Are there any sector-specific trends impacting the company (e.g., commodity prices, regulation)?
Regulatory trends like the Department of Labor's fiduciary rule changes could impact distribution fees and revenue sharing arrangements for asset managers like BlackRock. Increasing demand for sustainable/ESG investing strategies is also an industry trend BlackRock is capitalizing on.

Macroeconomic Exposures:
- How is the company exposed to macroeconomic factors such as inflation, interest rates, currency fluctuations, or trade policies?
As an asset manager, BlackRock's revenue is directly impacted by movements in global financial markets and asset prices. Rising inflation and interest rates could negatively impact asset valuations and fee revenue. Currency fluctuations impact the translation of BlackRock's international revenue and earnings.

- What is the impact of the current economic cycle on the company's business model?
BlackRock's business model is geared to benefit from bull markets and rising asset prices that drive AUM growth. However, its diversified product mix across active, index, and cash management strategies helps insulate it from the full impacts of bear markets. Overall, BlackRock is well-positioned to navigate economic cycles.

Risk Factors:
- What risks has the company highlighted, and how are they being managed?
Key risks highlighted by BlackRock include failure to capitalize on growth opportunities, underperformance of investment strategies, excessive outflows, operational/technology failures, regulatory changes, litigation, and inability to attract/retain talent. BlackRock aims to mitigate these risks through its diversified business mix, risk management, controls, and corporate culture.

- How is the company adapting to or capitalizing on macroeconomic changes?
BlackRock continues adapting its product lineup to investor demand, such as building out its illiquid alternatives and private markets capabilities. It is also investing in technology like Aladdin to enhance distribution and portfolio analytics offerings. Internationally, BlackRock is expanding in areas like China to capitalize on growth opportunities.

Corporate Development:
- What new projects, acquisitions, or partnerships has the company announced?
In Q2 2024, BlackRock completed the acquisition of SpiderRock Advisors to enhance its separately managed accounts business. It also announced acquisitions of GIP (infrastructure investing) and Preqin (private markets data/analytics) to bolster capabilities in key growth areas.

- How do these align with the company's long-term strategy? 
These acquisitions directly support BlackRock's strategic focus on expanding its higher-fee illiquid alternatives and private markets offerings, as well as enhancing its technology and analytics capabilities to better serve clients.

Capital Return:
