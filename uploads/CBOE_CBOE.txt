o How has the company's revenue changed compared to the previous quarter and the same quarter last year?
Total revenues increased 7% to $974.0 million in Q2 2024 compared to $907.8 million in Q2 2023. Compared to Q1 2024, total revenues increased 9% from $957.2 million.

o Is the growth in sales driven by volume or price increases? 
The revenue growth was primarily driven by an increase in trading volumes across several product lines, as well as an increase in the Section 31 fee rate. Specifically, index options ADV increased 9%, futures ADV increased 28%, and European equities ADNV increased 10% year-over-year. The increase in the Section 31 fee rate from $8.00 to $16.70 per million dollars of covered sales also boosted regulatory fee revenues.

The strong revenue growth indicates rising demand for Cboe's products and services across its global derivatives, securities and FX trading platforms. The increases in trading volumes suggest expanding market share and successful geographic expansion, particularly in Europe and Asia Pacific. This positive momentum could support further stock price appreciation if it continues.
